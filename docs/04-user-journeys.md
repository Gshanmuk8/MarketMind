# 04 · User Journeys

## J1 — Onboarding (the 60-second promise)

1. Sign up (email or OAuth). No questionnaire.
2. Enter one thing: `https://mycompany.com`.
3. Watch live progress: *Understanding your company → Mapping your market → Discovering competitors → Building your baseline*.
4. Review the AI's company profile (industry, ICP, features, pricing, keywords) — **editable**.
5. Review discovered competitors with confidence scores — accept / reject / add manually.
6. Land on a dashboard already populated with a baseline intelligence report (doc 11).

## J2 — The morning check (the habit loop)

1. Open dashboard → "Good morning" + Today's Intelligence.
2. Scan: threat meter, signal feed (each item shows *what changed → why it matters → recommended action*), AI model updates.
3. One tap into anything interesting; done in under five minutes.
4. Alternatively, the same content arrived as a digest on their channel at their chosen time (doc 12).

## J3 — Investigating a competitor

1. Competitors list, ranked by threat score.
2. Open a profile: overview, pricing, tech stack, AI models, GitHub activity, hiring, funding, sentiment, strengths/weaknesses, AI strategic summary.
3. Every claim labeled: verified fact vs AI inference (+ confidence).
4. Threat score expands into its factor breakdown — never a black box (doc 07, ScoreSnapshot).

## J4 — Making a decision (workspace → memory)

1. A signal or report raises a question ("Two competitors shipped voice — should we?").
2. Open the Decision Workspace (doc 14): frame the question, attach evidence (signals, insights), list options.
3. Ask the AI for a decision brief: evidence for/against each option, grounded in collected intelligence.
4. Decide. The decision is recorded in Decision Memory (doc 15) with rationale, evidence, rejected alternatives, and a revisit date.
5. Months later the platform flags: "The market moved against this decision — revisit?"

## J5 — Configuring delivery

1. Notifications page: add Email/Telegram, pick frequency + exact HH:MM time (own timezone), choose topics and minimum priority.
2. Set quiet hours, weekend pause, vacation mode. Critical-override is opt-in.

## J6 — Asking the strategist

1. Open Chat. Ask: "What should I build next?", "Compare us with Linear", "What pricing changed this month?"
2. Answers cite the underlying signals/insights and respect trust tiers.
3. Answers reference Decision Memory: "You considered this in March and chose X because Y."
